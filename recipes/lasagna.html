Classic Lasagna

Description

Lasagna layers pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta, and plenty of cheese. It is baked until bubbling and golden—comfort food that feeds a crowd and reheats well the next day.

This version keeps the structure classic: build the layers, bake covered, then uncover for a final pass to brown the top.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles (oven-ready or boiled until al dente)
  • 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 28 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 egg (optional, to bind ricotta)

Steps

  1. Brown the meat with onion and garlic; stir in tomatoes, paste, and spices; simmer 20–30 minutes.
  2. Mix ricotta with egg, salt, and half the Parmesan if using.
  3. Spread a thin layer of sauce in a baking dish, then alternate noodles, ricotta, sauce, and mozzarella.
  4. Finish with sauce and remaining cheeses on top.
  5. Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 45 minutes; uncover and bake until browned, about 15 minutes more.
  6. Rest 10–15 minutes before slicing.
