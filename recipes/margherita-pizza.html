Margherita Pizza

Description

Pizza Margherita is the Neapolitan classic: a thin crust topped with tomato, fresh mozzarella, basil, and olive oil. The colors echo the Italian flag and the flavors stay simple and bright.

A very hot oven (or pizza steel/stone) gives the crust leopard spotting and a chewy interior—aim for minimal toppings so the dough can shine.

Ingredients

  • 1 ball pizza dough (about 250 g), room temperature
  • ⅓ cup tomato sauce (passata or crushed tomatoes, lightly salted)
  • 4–6 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced or torn
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Fine salt
  • Semolina or flour for dusting the peel

Steps

  1. Preheat oven as hot as it goes (500°F+ / 260°C+) with a stone or steel inside if you have one.
  2. Stretch or roll the dough into a 10–12 inch round, leaving a slightly thicker rim.
  3. Transfer to a floured or semolina-dusted peel or inverted baking sheet.
  4. Spread sauce lightly, add mozzarella, a pinch of salt, and a drizzle of oil.
  5. Bake until the crust is puffed and spotted and cheese is bubbling, about 7–10 minutes.
  6. Top with basil and another light drizzle of oil; slice and serve immediately.
