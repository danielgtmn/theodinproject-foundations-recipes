Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

These are the familiar drop cookies with butter, brown sugar, and plenty of chocolate chips. The edges stay crisp while the centers stay soft when you pull them from the oven slightly underbaked.

Chill the dough if you have time—it reduces spread and deepens flavor—or bake right away for thinner, crisper cookies.

Ingredients

  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips or chunks

Steps

  1. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Cream butter with both sugars until light; beat in eggs and vanilla.
  3. Stir in dry ingredients until just combined; fold in chocolate.
  4. Drop rounded tablespoons onto lined baking sheets, spaced apart.
  5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 9–11 minutes until edges are golden.
  6. Cool on the sheet a few minutes, then transfer to a rack.
